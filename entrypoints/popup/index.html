What is MonkeyMeter?

MonkeyMeter is an autopilot drift detector that helps you become aware of mindless browsing patterns. It tracks behavioral signals like distracting content usage (like youtube shorts, tiktok, etc) and dwell time to compute a real-time focus score.

Instead of blocking sites, it uses reflective prompts to help you pause and decide: "Am I here intentionally, or is the monkey brain in control?"

Inspiration

This project draws inspiration from Tim Urban's legendary TED talk, where he describes the “instant gratification monkey” that hijacks our focus and fuels procrastination.

Rather than treating screen time as inherently bad, MonkeyMeter focuses on intentionality. Educational content, deep work, and purposeful browsing don't count against you — only autopilot drift does.

How It Works

1.
Behavioral Signal Detection The extension monitors two primary signals: media content (such as videos/shorts/games) that are detected to be distracting and dwell drift (time spent on unproductive sites exceeding 30 seconds).
2.
Weighted Event System Each behavioral event is assigned a weight (0-1 scale) based on its severity. Short-form content starts at 0.3 weight. Dwell drift weight scales with time: 30s = 0.4, 60s = 0.7, 90s+ = 1.0. Educational content gets zero weight.
3.
Exponential Moving Average (EMA) Events are aggregated every 5 minutes using EMA smoothing (α = 0.3) to prevent score volatility. This creates a smoothed autopilot score that reacts to sustained patterns, not isolated actions.
4.
Focus Score Computation Your focus score (0-100) is calculated as: 100 - (smoothed_score × 100). The score is computed across three rolling time windows: 5m, 25m, and 1h, with trend indicators (↑↓→) to show direction. Simply stopping drift behavior allows your score to naturally recover. As old events expire from the rolling window, your focus score climbs back up.
5.
State Machine with Hysteresis Five monkey states (🧘 Focused → 🐒 Curious → 🙈 Distracted → 🙊 Doom Mode → 🐵 Chaos) are determined by score thresholds. Hysteresis prevents rapid state flipping: entering a state requires crossing a lower threshold, exiting requires crossing a higher one.

Key Features

🎓
Educational Content Detection Pages are automatically classified as educational by scanning titles and metadata for keywords like "tutorial," "documentation," "course," etc. Educational domains are excluded from drift scoring entirely.
💭
Reflective Overlay Prompts When your score drops below threshold (indicating sustained drift), an overlay appears asking: "Still intentional?" You can confirm intentionality, acknowledge monkey mode, or suppress future prompts for that domain.

Privacy

All tracking, scoring, and session data lives exclusively in local browser storage.
